About

Dr. Sarabjeet Singh graduated in 2011 from Shere Kashmir university of agricultural sciences and technology (SKUAST-J) as a Veterinarian. Then he worked in 3 different shelters for 2 years (doing more than 4000 surgeries) where he got commited to devoting his life for treatment of animals. This was my awakening as a vet.

For the past 3 years Dr. Sarabjeet has been working as vet at Dog and Cat Clinic, Kalkaji, Delhi. Recently, he started his own Clinic in Jammu where he hails from. For first 20 Days of every month Dr. Sarabjeet works in Delhi and last 10 days of every month he works at his clinic in Jammu.

About Dirtyvet

The inspiration behind the name of the website was Dr. Sarabjeet's experience of working in Animal Shelters at the beginning of his career where the working conditions were quite dirty. Working in dirty conditions is a part of the job which Dr. Sarabjeet embraces.

Emergency Dystocia Surgery

Dr Sarabjeet and his associate Maan Bhadur performing a midnight emergency surgery on a stray bitch at a shelter. The team got a call at midnight about her being in very bad condition. After her examination Dr. Sarabjeet observed that she was pregnant and had dystocia. She was not putting any effort to deliver. Dr. Sarabjeet and Maan Bahadur worked overnight and saved her and 2 puppies. The remaining 4 puppies were still births. She was neutered in process.

  • Patient: Stray Bitch
  • Date: December 2012
  • Service: Surgical Delivery

Monkey Arm Amputation

A stray infant monkey came with totally necrosed arm due to an electric shock and had maggots in that arm. This infant's arm was amputated. He recovered completely only to escape the shelter. Locals who saw him on trees fed him and he became a local hero making Dr. Sarabjeet famous as well.

  • Patient: Stray Infant Monkey
  • Date: March 2013
  • Service: Arm Amputation

Leg Amputation

Amputation of a necrosed leg of a old age stray dog who was hit and run over by unknown person. With drain water in wound and necrosis Dr. Sarabjeet had no other option but to amputate him. As he was old, everyone thought he will not survive however Dr. Sarabjeet was taught and guided by a good vet from USA who was taught by an Australian vet to do blood less amputation at joints which helped me to save him.

  • Patient: Aged Dog
  • Date: April 2014
  • Service: Leg Amputation

Stray dog rehablitation

A stray dog with maggots in it's ear came late at night with extreme pain and discomfort so Dr. Sarabjeet Singh along with very good dedicated staff at the aminal shelter sedated him and removed his maggots. A treatment with combination of painkillers, antibiotics and antihistaminics recovered strength of the stray dog and saved him. The team released him after 15 days from the shelter.

  • Patient: Stray Dog
  • Date: April 2014
  • Service: Rehablitation

Lipoma Removal

Intussusception Treatment in adverse conditions

A stray pup in foster care case was suffering from intussusception for the past 10 days. Dr. Sarabjeet lacked equipment to do surgery so he improvised with whatever equipment that was available to him. Dr. Sarabjeet performed strict asepsis which saved the dog's life.

  • Patient: Dog
  • Date: June 2015
  • Service: Intussusception treatment
